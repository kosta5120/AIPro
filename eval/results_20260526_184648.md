# RAG Ablation Study — 20260526_184648

**Load mode:** `full` (one document per PDF, RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter handles chunking)

## Experiment 1 — Top-k: 3 vs 5 vs 8 (dense retrieval)

Keeps the dense index identical and varies only how many chunks are passed to the LLM, trading recall against context noise.

## Experiment 2 — Retrieval strategy: dense vs BM25 vs hybrid (k=5)

Same chunk corpus, three different retrieval algorithms:
- **dense**: cosine similarity over `bge-large-en-v1.5` embeddings (semantic match)
- **BM25**: lexical match with term-frequency saturation (wins on named entities)
- **hybrid**: Reciprocal Rank Fusion of dense + BM25 top-20 pools (no score normalization needed)

*Note on chunking:* chunks are produced by LangChain's `RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter(chunk_size=500, chunk_overlap=50)` (character-based) operating on the full concatenated PDF text.
